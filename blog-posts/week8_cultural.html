1. Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

My friend from college used to make jokes about me from time to time in front of me and others.  It was funny for him and others around him, but I didn't find it very funny because it happened frequently.

2. What was the source of the conflict?

See above.

3. How did you handle that conflict?

I didn't handle it very well in the end.  I just didn't say anything, kept it to myself, and bottled up all the negative emotions.  Sadly, emotional conflict transformed into physical conflict in the end.

4. Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

It made it worse.

5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

If I could go back, I think I would have not bottled up all the negative emotions.  Instead, I would try to exude discomfort and let him know how I felt at the time.

When I look back now, I am not sure why I let those types of things get to me.  I feel that I am such a much different person now than who I was five years ago.  In the recent years, I have been used to dividing emotional conflicts into two types: the one that will affect me personally and one that is not.  Given that this was an ongoing today, I would have totally brushed this off instantly and I wouldn't even be frustrated.

6. What did you learn from this experience?

I learned that I should not bottle up anger and frustration.  I should, instead, identify the issue and try to resolve it.
